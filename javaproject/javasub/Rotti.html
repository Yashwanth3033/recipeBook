Butter Nan

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • Extra butter for brushing
  • Chopped cilantro (optional)
Instructions:
  1. In a small bowl, mix the yeast, sugar, and warm milk. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Make a well in the center and add the yogurt, melted butter, and the yeast mixture. Mix to form a dough.
  4. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let it rise for 1-2 hours, or until doubled in size.
  5. Preheat a skillet or tandoor over medium-high heat.
  6. Divide the dough into small balls and roll each ball into a flat oval or circle.
  7. Cook each naan on the skillet for about 1-2 minutes on each side or until brown spots appear. You can also use a tandoor for a more authentic flavor.
  8. Brush the hot naan with melted butter and sprinkle with chopped cilantro if desired.
  9. Serve warm with your favorite curry or dip.

Aloo Paratha

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 cup boiled and mashed potatoes
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee for cooking
Instructions:
  1. In a large bowl, mix the whole wheat flour with enough water to form a smooth dough. Cover and set aside.
  2. In another bowl, combine the mashed potatoes with cumin seeds, coriander powder, garam masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, green chilies, cilantro, and salt. Mix well.
  3. Divide the dough into small balls and roll each ball into a small circle.
  4. Place a spoonful of the potato mixture in the center of the circle. Fold the edges over the filling and seal it to form a ball.
  5. Roll out the stuffed dough ball gently into a flat circle, being careful not to let the filling escape.
  6. Heat a tava or skillet over medium heat. Place the rolled paratha on the skillet and cook until brown spots appear on both sides, applying a little oil or ghee as needed.
  7. Serve hot with yogurt, pickles, or your favorite curry.
