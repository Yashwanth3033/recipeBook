Panner Veg Biryani

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 200 grams paneer, cubed
  • 1 large onion, finely sliced
  • 1 large tomato, chopped
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp biryani masala
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
Instructions:
  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a large pan. Add cumin seeds, mustard seeds, and let them splutter.
  3. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown. Add minced garlic and ginger, and sauté for another minute.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes, green chilies, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and biryani masala. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil separates.
  5. Add mixed vegetables and cook for 5-7 minutes. Stir in the paneer cubes and cook for another 2 minutes.
  6. Add yogurt and mix well. Cook for another 2 minutes.
  7. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil and add the soaked rice. Cook the rice until it's 70% done. Drain the water.
  8. Layer the cooked rice over the vegetable and paneer mixture in the pan. Sprinkle chopped coriander and mint leaves on top.
  9. Cover the pan with a lid and cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  10. Remove from heat and let it rest for 5 minutes before serving. Fluff the biryani with a fork and serve hot.

Veg Palav

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Basmati rice
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, peas, etc.)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 green chili, slit
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil or ghee
  • 2 cups water
  • Coriander leaves for garnish
Instructions:
  1. Rinse the Basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak it in water for 20-30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a large pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle.
  3. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chili. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Cook until the tomatoes soften.
  6. Add mixed vegetables and cook for 5 minutes until they are slightly tender.
  7. Add the soaked rice to the pan. Mix gently to combine with the spices and vegetables.
  8. Pour in 2 cups of water and add salt. Stir well.
  9. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pan and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and water is absorbed.
  10. Turn off the heat and let the pulao sit covered for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork.
  11. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves before serving.
